## Mitte

[Brandenburg Gate](geo:52.516392119744445,13.377671913529088)
Arguably the most symbolic structure in Berlin.

[Bebelplatz](geo:52.51690849396063,13.395574532854518)
Large public square on the Unter den Linden, including St Hedwig's Cathedral and the State Opera House. A good place for pictures.

[Deutscher Dom](geo:52.51275209485799,13.392555183662504)
The cathedral of Berlin. It no longer does religious services and is operated by the Bundestag with displays about German parliamentary democracy.

[Memorial to the Murdered Jews of Europe](geo:52.51433538853183,13.37863527608666)
Holocaust memorial. It's very striking. Nearby are other Holocaust memorials for other communities persecuted by the Nazis.

[Fernsehturm](geo:52.520939035791734,13.409413735459795)
The large TV tower in Alexanderplatz. It was originally a broadcasting tower for East German TV (which are still in operation today!) It is seen as a symbol of the GDR since reunification. Nowadays, there is a really mediocre revolving restaurant and a viewing platform which, increasingly, you can see less of the city.

[Weltzeituhr](geo:52.521278750361816,13.41335600846443)
A large clock split into 24 sections showing the time around the world, built by the communists and it shows...

[Rotes Rathaus](geo:52.51843418578061,13.408617477761918)
Berlin City Hall which is very impressive. There's a few exhibitions inside.

[Reichstag Building](geo:52.51875729901935,13.376192564300522)
The building of the Bundestag, the Parliament of the German Federal Government. There is a glass dome design by Norman Foster which you can visit with multiple slots per day. Wednesday is typically the Bundestag's Plenary which you can sit in. It's completely free.

[Soviet War Memorial](geo:52.516890020100355,13.3718696365342)
One of the three memorials erected by the Soviet Union to commemorate its war dead. A treaty between Germany and Russia mandates the German government keep it in a good state of repair.

[Neue Synagoge](geo:52.525001658704035,13.394119691315407)
Built in the 1800s, it is a stunning building that has somehow survived the Nazis and the war. There is a museum about its history (especially during the Nazi era) and you can access the dome.

## Charlottenburg, Wilmersdorf and Schöneberg

[Schloss Charlottenburg](geo:52.52114877433059,13.295669233144757)
A baroque summer palace with a large gardens. You can visit the palace itself where there are rooms with huge paintings and tapestries

[Kaiser-Wilhelm Memorial Church](geo:52.504926737836584,13.335107902917342)
A memorial church to Kaiser Wilhelm I (not the last one!) It was built between 1891 and 1906 but badly damaged during the war. Rather than rebuilt, a modern place of worship was built and the remains of the tower serve as an exhibition place. Both are free to visit.

[Gedenkstätte Plötzensee _Plötzensee Prison_](geo:52.54095885888448,13.322771813827067)
A somewhat forgotten memorial to the victims of the Nazi's in the place of a former execution room where nearly 2,900 people were executed during the regime. Next to the real, operational prison of the same name.

[Olympiastadion](geo:52.51537611755836,13.239070627339224)
Built by the Nazis for the 1936 Olympics. Neoclassical and indicative of fascist aesthetics. Current home to Hertha BSC club.

[Nollendorfplatz](geo:52.49975519825933,13.35409858007758)
Berlin's gay quarter, if such a thing can exist in a city like Berlin.

## East Central (Kreuzberg, Friedrichshain, Prenzlauer Berg)

[Checkpoint Charlie](geo:52.507597322700384,13.390417010154422)
The site of a the only border crossing between East and West Berlin that allowed passage for foreigners (but not Berliners). The guardhouse here is a replica but the signage is authentic. Included here performatively - it really is not worth going out of your way for and it's really popular with tourists (especially American tourists)

[East Side Gallery](geo:52.50524446770854,13.439793212476937)
The longest stretch of the Berlin Wall still in existence. These murals are painted on the East Side so are not from the Cold War. Free to visit.

## East (Lichtenberg, Marzahn-Hellersdorf and Pankow)

[Gärten der Welt](geo:52.53849533875176,13.569269403188711)
Park with a number of different gardens built by cultures around the world - some of which are authentically curated by partnerships between Berlin and other cities. A bit of a trek out of the city centre - accessible by the M8 tram from Alexanderplatz as well as other tram services.

## Tempelhof and Neukölln

[Tempelhof Airport](geo:52.482644066423994,13.389260405779453)
A former airport which is now a park, museum and event space featured in many movies. It's a site of lots of politics given it is a large open space in an area with a housing shortage but ballot measures cannot pass to repurpose the space.
